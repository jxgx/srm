Oral Examinations

Oral Examinations capture a simple physical action. The action is the opening of the mouth. The mouth opens. The head may or may not be tipped back into position. The camera gazes at the mouth, picking up information as presented in the teeth, the lips, the gums, the tongue, the uvula, and even into the inner throat. The mouth remains open for an extended amount of time that varies based on the physical presence and ability of the performer. Oral Examinations present an extended glimpse of the body. Viewers often express a kind of calming feeling, although other viewers feel a direct rush of confrontation when faced with the pure psycho-physicality of the female mouth. Gazing upon the mouth for an extended period of time transforms the body from physical object to an inhuman static.
